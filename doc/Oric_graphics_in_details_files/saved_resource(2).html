        Pretty neat exposé there, just saw a youtube rundown of 50 random Oric games in a half hour and was wondering how come there was such a stark difference in graphical quality between the original 1980s games and some of the more modern homebrews. The latter ones all having that characteristic AIC flavour to them. Seemed like an entirely different video mode.

        Though I do wonder if there's some other hidden magic somewhere with colour emphasis or the like. Even some of the 80s games seemed to have more than 8 colours, without using dithering or alternate-line colour switching. Particularly one maze race game where the walls were made up of a double line of full intensity primary blue bordered with half brightness navy blue, and the cars themselves had slightly pastel, slightly desaturated colours... pinkish rather than full red or magneta, and a sort of minty green rather than full green or cyan, without being the puce or aqua that a combination of the primaries would produce. More like the TV saturation had been backed off and the brightness turned up a little, but it only affected sprites or text characters with those particular colours rather than being full frame...

            Regarding the games of the 80ies, there's a major difference depending of if you were using the SCART or antenna output.

            With SCART you got some pretty neat squares of color, while on antenna there is a huge amount of bluryness, and colors just look plain different.

            I did not realize that until I saw a few Oric in UK, and saw that the display was so radically different, which kind of explained why French games tended to focus on the graphics and the british games on the actual gameplay: Nobody in france was using the antenna output because it was not compatible with our tvs, so we had to use SCART, making each pixel super noticeable.

            On UK systems it just looked like washed out mush :D

            Would be interesting to know which game you were referring to
